A Sanctuary Among 300 Distractions: Why We Built an App That Doesn't Judge You

Statistics suggest we check our smartphones nearly 300 times a day. Each time, we are met with a barrage of notifications, demands, and noise—constantly performing for the world, yet feeling more depleted.

When we designed the Kamidana App, we wanted to create a counter-balance: a digital sanctuary that allows you to return to your center for just one minute out of those 300 distractions.

1. A Presence Without Judgment

In the app's backyard, you will encounter a small fox. It speaks in quiet, child-like reactions, but it never asks for your attention or evaluates your progress.

The core philosophy of this fox is to be a "non-judgmental presence that maintains a comfortable distance." In a digital world where everything is rated and judged, having a companion that accepts you exactly as you are—without intruding on your space—is a rare and vital luxury.

2. The Single Daily "Call to Mindfulness"

Most apps compete for your attention dozens of times a day. We intentionally chose to ring the bell only once.

Once a day, we send a gentle nudge: "Time to refresh your offerings." This isn't a nagging alarm; it's a "Call to Prayer." Among the hundreds of times you check your phone for work or social media, this one alert is for you—a signal to step away from the noise and enter your private sanctuary.

3. Carrying Your Sanctuary Everywhere

Our technology should serve our humanity, not exploit it. By tending to your altar and spending a silent minute with the fox, you engage in a deliberate act of intentionality.

Kamidana App is not about productivity; it is a portable home for your heart. It’s a place where you are never judged, and where peace is always just one tap away.
